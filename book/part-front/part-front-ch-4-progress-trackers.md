# Chapter 4: Progress Trackers

---

#### 4.1 Introduction to the Progress Trackers

The progress trackers provide a structured system for self-monitoring throughout the exegesis. Each tracker is organized by section and phase, allowing the reader to track completion at a granular level. The trackers serve three primary purposes:

1. **Orientation:** They provide a clear overview of what remains to be completed.
2. **Motivation:** They offer a visual record of progress, reinforcing momentum.
3. **Reflection:** They help identify bottlenecks and areas requiring additional attention.

The trackers are designed to be used iteratively. The reader should update them after each session, marking tasks as complete only when they are genuinely finished.

---

#### 4.2 Tracker Iconography and Conventions

Each tracker uses the following iconography:

| Icon | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- |
| ☐ | Not started |
| ◐ | In progress |
| ☑ | Completed |

**Guidelines for Use:**

- **Honesty:** Mark a task as complete only when it is genuinely done. Partial work should be marked as "In progress" (◐).
- **Consistency:** Update the tracker after each session. Do not let multiple sessions pass without updating.
- **Reflection:** When a task remains incomplete for an extended period, reflect on why. Is the material too difficult? Is the pace too fast? Adjust accordingly.

#### 4.7 Reflection and Adjustment Protocol

When a task remains incomplete for an extended period, the following reflection protocol should be applied:

1. **Identify the Bottleneck:** Is the material too difficult? Is the pace too fast? Are there external constraints? Does a Zone 2 skeleton contain notation errors that failed the QC check?

2. **Adjust the Approach:** Consider re-reading the prerequisite material, consulting supplementary texts (Treatise or Heaviside), or breaking the task into smaller sub-tasks. If the math-visualization loop is blocked, check the parameter values in the computation cell.

3. **Document the Adjustment:** Note the adjustment in the Raw Annotations Workspace for the relevant section.

4. **Reassess:** After the adjustment, reassess progress and update the tracker accordingly.

**Reflection Questions:**

- What specific concept or operation is causing difficulty?
- Have the prerequisite foundations been fully mastered?
- Is the visualization phase revealing gaps in the mathematical understanding?
- Did the Zone 2 QC check identify anachronisms that must be edited before reading?
- Would consulting the Treatise or Heaviside provide clarity?
